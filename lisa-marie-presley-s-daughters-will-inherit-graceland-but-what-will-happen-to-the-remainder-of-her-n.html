What Happened To Lisa Marie Presley?

Lisa Marie Presley was born on February 1, 1968, in Memphis, Tennessee. Growing up in the shadow of her father's fame, Lisa Marie's childhood was not without its struggles, including her parent's divorce when she was just four years old.

Despite this, Presley inherited her father's musical talent and began writing and recording her own music as a young adult. She has released several albums over the years, including her debut album "To Whom It May Concern," which was certified gold in the United States.

In addition to her successful music and acting career, Presley was involved in various philanthropic endeavors over the years. She supported charities such as the Dream Factory, which helps fulfill the final wishes of terminally ill adults, and the Presley Charitable Foundation, which provides assistance to organizations focused on children's education, health, and welfare.

Lisa Marie Presley was vocal about her struggles with addiction and mental health, using her platform and her book to raise awareness about these issues and encourage others to seek help.

Presley was famously married to the pop icon Michael Jackson in the late 1990s and early 2000s, and their high-profile relationship was closely scrutinized by the press. She has also been in the spotlight for her struggles with addiction and her highly publicized divorces, including from musician Danny Keough and actor Nicolas Cage.

Why Did Priscilla Presley Contest Lisa Marie's Will?

BBC News reported that Priscilla Presley, the ex-wife of the late rock and roll icon Elvis Presley and Lisa Marie's mother has contested the validity of the will of her late daughter and has thus filed a challenge in court.

The legal challenge is disputing a 2016 amendment made to Lisa Marie's will, which removed Priscilla as the estate trustee and replaced her with Lisa Marie's two children, Riley and Benjamin Keough.

Even though Benjamin Keough died in 2020, this has made Riley the sole trustee named in the amendment. Priscilla's lawyers argue that the amendment is invalid, they cited several issues including a misspelling of her name and an unusual signature on the will claimed to be Lisa Marie's.

According to BBC, they also claim that Priscilla was not made aware of the amendment during her daughter's lifetime, which would have been required by the will. This challenge could result in Priscilla being reinstated as the estate trustee and regaining control over her daughter's assets.
