Only a few of the go-green stars go beyond the usual activism posts on social media, proving that being an environmental activist isn't a walk in the park. Leading immersive community events and pioneering climate change awareness campaigns forms part of their busy lives off the set.

They not only preach about climate solutions; they actively implement the suggestions through endorsements and donations. They are going far out of their comfort zones to ensure the world is kind to the environment. These charismatic actors top the list of the most charitable environmentalists you've heard about.

LEONARDO DICAPRIO

This Titanic star has more to his name than the Oscar Awards and his looks. His initiatives towards securing a brighter future for earthlings are immense. He is the founder of Leonardo DiCaprio Foundation, and he recently joined with Laurene Powell Jobs and Brian Sheth to form a new environmental alliance called Earthly Alliance.

The new organization seeks to track climate change and protect biodiversity worldwide, starting with the most affected areas. Through his foundation, he has donated over $30 million towards initiatives that promote climate change awareness.

ED BEGLEY, JR

Ed Begley Jr. has made conservation so much a part of his life that he has attained Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) Platinum status. This certification is not easy to come by. It is seen in the actor's commitment to a greener future, considering he rarely gets on a plane.

Begley is a vegan, drives an electric car, uses biodegradable soap, decomposes his kitchen waste, and his house is nothing short of renewable energy sources. He confirms that going green was the best financial decision he ever made. The world should borrow a leaf from him.

ROBERT REDFORD

The recently retired actor, director and founder of Sundance Institute continues to oversee his business and champion environmental campaigns at 83. Together with his three children, he founded the Redford Center, which is a non-profit company that produces environmental impact films.

He was honored at the 2014 Goldman Prize alongside narrating the recipient profile videos at the awarding ceremony. He is also a trustee of the board of Natural Resources Defense Council. He has pushed for the implementation of environmental laws aimed at increasing recycling and reducing energy consumption.

ED NORTON

The superstar used his fame to involve the public in fighting for this worthy cause actively. His environmental advocacy stems from his father, who was a life-long advocate for a sustainable future.

Through the Maasai Wilderness Conservation Trust, he is working with a Maasai warrior, Samson Parashina, to help the community located in southern Kenya in realizing the economic output of their natural resources. Norton is also the first United Nations Goodwill Ambassador for Biodiversity and founder of Crowdrise.com that mobilizes funds in support of environmental initiatives.

DARYL HANNAH

Having autism since childhood has not stopped the 52-year-old actress from becoming an overachiever in her charitable works. Her impactful contributions towards sustainable living earned her the 2011 Environmental Hero award at the Santa Barbara Earth Day Festival.

The vegan activist has a website dedicated to encouraging sustainable living and low impact lifestyles. In addition to her outspoken activism and establishing the Sustainable Biodiesel Alliance, her home is solar powered with rain harvesting. Moreover, she doesn't use petroleum products on any of her vehicles.

SHAILENE WOODLEY

You probably remember her as the smiley teen who played Hazel in The Fault in Our Stars. She uses her celebrity status to advocate for environmental justice and social change. Her stand is that ecological conservation has a lot of impact on our mental health as earthlings.

She has partnered with American Express in a bid to save the oceans from pollution. The actress who, at the moment, lives on the road traveling, plans to build an eco-friendly and sustainable house for herself to support her cause.

OLIVIA WILDE

The entrepreneurial actress and producer couldn't have used her fame in a better way. While a million celebrities are out there launching vegan fashion lines, Wilde has found a way to bring environmentalism to your closet.

She believes she can save animals as well as the soil from textile industry waste through her Conscious Commerce Initiative. In April, she launched the Choose Used a limited-edition collection by H&M, which comprises of used clothes sold online in partnership with thredUP. The activist also tries as much as she can to shop and dress in vintage clothes.

GISELE BUNDCHEN

The gorgeous Brazilian model is a goodwill ambassador for the United Nations Environment Program. Since her retirement from modeling, she has been working on global environmental issues that are affecting the climate.

Her global presence in the push for sustainability was recognized in London at the 2011 International Green awards, where she received the Greenest Celebrity Award. Her most impactful contributions include the Agua Limpa project that she and her family spearheaded for the re-afforestation of the Amazon forest.

AL GORE

Being the former Vice President, Al Gore is an outspoken environmentalist who uses his political fame to spread awareness on climate change. His philanthropic efforts in climate change activism earned him the coveted Nobel Peace prize in 2007.

Through his films, An Inconvenient Truth in 2006 with its sequel Truth to Power 2017, he documents the efforts he made to persuade the government to invest in renewable energy sources as well as the progress made so far in the green movement.

GEORGE CLOONEY

His activism has been met with a lot of controversies. Environmentalists are calling the A-List actor a Hollywood hypocrite for his empty talks on climate change, yet he leaves an enormous carbon footprint with his lifestyle. Despite the criticism, the Hollywood star has done quite a lot to better things for our future.

Apart from driving an electric car, he is leading the green movement through the Oil Change campaign that aims at eliminating US dependence on imported oil. Well, he is evidently walking the talk.
