Did No Doubt's Success Lead To Gwen Stefani's Family And Divorce Drama?

Highlights

  • Gwen Stefani admits she regrets spending too much time away from her family due to her work with No Doubt.
  • Stefani felt like she was failing as a musician and a mother while trying to balance both roles.
  • Despite Stefani's guilt, Gavin Rossdale's affair with the family nanny likely led to their dramatic divorce.

Thanks to Gwen Stefani's time with No Doubt and her years as a solo artist, she is one of the biggest music superstars of her generation. That level of success has also allowed Stefani to accumulate a truly impressive fortune she is still adding to.

Sadly for Stefani, however, she admitted that being a member of No Doubt took her away from her family. With that news in mind, some fans have been wondering what role that could have played in Stefani and Gavin Rossdale's dramatic divorce.

This article will reveal what Gwen Stefani revealed about how being a member of No Doubt took her away from her family. Then, the article will look at what Stefani and Gavin Rossdale have said about what led to their divorce.

Gwen Stefani Thinks She Spent Too Much Time Away From Her Children Because Of No Doubt

In April 2024, Gwen Stefani spoke to Nylon Mag about her life and career. During one very revealing section of the interview, Stefani spoke about how hard it was for her to strike a balance between her work and personal life.

Most specifically, Stefani talked to the outlet about regrets she has from the time she was working on No Doubt's final album to date, 2012's "Push and Shove". As Stefani explained, she now believes she should have spent more time with her family at that time instead of working on the album.

"God, what was I doing trying to please everybody?’ Because really, I should have just been with my family."

Stefani also went on to talk to Nylon's interviewer about how she felt like she wasn't satisfying anyone or herself at that time. Trying to split her time between being a musician and a mother sadly made her feel like she was failing at both things.

“I would be leaving my family. And if I didn’t come home with a song, I’d be like, ‘Oh my God, I’m such a loser — I didn’t have dinner with my family, and I didn’t write a song. I wasted an entire day of my life trying to be in No Doubt again.’”

Still, it is a good thing for someone like Stefani, who seems like she has everything together, to be honest about feeling the same way.

Even though Stefani told Nylon that she is happy with the songs on No Doubt's 2012 album, she made it clear that it was a painful time in her life to remember.

"But we did it, and there are some good songs. It was... Don’t remind me. I don’t want to talk about it.”

No Doubt Likely Had Very Little To Do With Gwen Stefani And Gavin Rossdale's Divorce

As anyone who has been in a long-term relationship will already know, couples are complicated. With that in mind, there is no way to definitively state that Stefani's work with No Doubt didn't contribute to her divorce in any way. After all, based on Stefani's 2024 comments, she clearly felt guilty about being away from her family and that could have affected things.

That said, based on what has been publicly reported on what led to Stefani and Gavin Rossdale's divorce, their split seems to have happened because of something he did.

An article from The Things revealed that in November 2015, US Weekly reported that Rossdale had a three-year affair with the family nanny, Mindy Mann. Part of US Weekly's report also focused on how Stefani reportedly learned about the affair in an extremely dramatic fashion.

According to US Weekly's reporting which cited the statements of an insider, the family's iPad was linked to Rossdale's phone. When one of Rossdale and Stefani's other nannies was using the iPad, they reportedly discovered unclothed photos of Mann and showed Stefani.

The family insider claims that Rossdale initially claimed that his relationship with Mann didn't go beyond flirtation. However, Rossdale would later admit to carrying on an affair with the nanny which left Stefani feeling "mortified, livid, and embarrassed" according to the insider.

Months later, in July 2016, Stefani spoke to Harper's Bazaar about what was going on in her career and personal life at the time. While talking to the outlet, Stefani spoke about how her son Apollo was eleven months old when "everything happened".

Harper's Bazaar's article that stemmed from their 2016 interview with Stefani then states that the "everything" the singer was talking about was discovering Rossdale's affair.

The article then quotes Stefani stating, "Everybody knows what happened next" and naming the date her life changed forever as "February 9". It is also worth noting that Stefani commented on how vividly she remembers the day her life changed. "I obviously know the date."

Stefani also explained to Harper's Bazaar that she spent months trying to get to the bottom of a secret after that date. "It was the beginning of hell. Like six, seven, eight months of torture, trying to figure out this big secret."

While Stefani doesn't explicitly state that she was trying to learn everything about Rossdale's affair, that is the obvious implication. Stefani also went on to talk to Harper's Bazaar about how her family was ripped apart after that. "It was so insane because not only did my family break up, but then my kids are taken away like half the time, so that was really like, 'What?! What did I do?'"

While talking to Fabulous magazine in 2017, Gavin Rossdale stated that he never wanted to get divorced from Stefani at all. “[Divorce] was completely opposite to what I wanted. Really not…but here we are.”

Since Rossdale didn't want the divorce and Stefani pretty much blamed his affair, it seems pretty definitive that his infidelity caused the drama. That also makes it seem all but certain that Stefani's work with No Doubt wasn't a notable cause for the divorce.
