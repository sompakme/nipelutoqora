Can Christine Baumgartner Support Her Lifestyle Without Kevin Costner?

Christine Baumgartner is an avid entrepreneur, having co-founded the renowned Cat Bag Couture handbag line, and launched her own luxury handbag collection. The 49-year-old's net worth is currently estimated to be an impressive $7 million. However, despite her successful entrepreneurial ventures, the former model has admitted to being incapable of supporting the kind of lifestyle her kids had gotten accustomed to during her 18-year marriage to Kevin Costner.

Baumgartner reportedly has no income of her own, having chosen to be a stay-at-home mom since the birth of her eldest child. The 49-year-old is, therefore, demanding child support payment amounting to a staggering $248,000 a month, which she claims is "less than the amount needed to maintain the children in their accustomed lifestyle."

However, Costner has been reluctant to remit the specified amount, claiming that his ex-wife intends to use the funds for herself, and not their kids. "Christine allocates 60 percent of expenses such as private trainer, unallocated credit card expenses, and her plastic surgery to the minor children without any explanation or basis."

Costner wrote in court documents obtained by US Weekly. "The children do not use the services of private trainers, only Christine does. The plastic surgery expenses of $188,500/month belong to Christine – not the children.”

The Yellowstone star has also admitted to being incapable of paying the requested amount, claiming that the current child support arrangement, which entitles Christine to $51,490/ month is more reasonable.

The Yellowstone alum went ahead to label his estranged ex-wife’s request “astonishing,” claiming that he couldn’t “afford to pay those amounts and pay [his] living and business expenses without liquidating assets.”
