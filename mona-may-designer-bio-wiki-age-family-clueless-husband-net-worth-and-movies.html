Mona May Costume Designer

She landed her first official costume design job in 1989 for the MTV comedy series, “Just Say Julie,” written by and starring Julie Brown. Her credits on the big and small screens include: L.A. nightlife spoof “A Night at the Roxbury”, Amy Adams as a Disney cartoon princess come to life in “Enchanted,” the television version of “Clueless” and the Whitney Houston TV movie biopic, “Whitney.”

She’s regularly challenging herself, incorporating live-action with CG or animation, or both  in “Enchanted,” “Stuart Little 2” and Eddie Murphy’s “The Haunted Mansion.”

For over 20 years, May has collaborated with actress and producer Drew Barrymore, beginning with ’80s-inspired looks in “The Wedding Singer,” turn-of-the-millennium ‘fits in “Never Been Kissed,”, and three seasons of SoCal style in the zombie comedy series “The Santa Clarita Diet.”

Mona May Clueless

She was the designer behind the outfits on the iconic film Clueless. May is the artist behind Cher’s red Alaïa dress, plaid suits, and 60 costume changes. Clueless is a 1995 American coming-of-age teen comedy film. The film recently celebrated its 25th anniversary.

May also has a presence on TikTok. Going through the video-sharing app and you’ll discover Gen Z dancing and lip-syncing in sheer tops, A-line skirts, and plaid prints, unknowingly imitating the style May created on the set of Clueless 25 years ago.

Mona May Enchanted

Enchanted is a 2007 American live-action/animated musical fantasy romantic comedy film, produced by Walt Disney Pictures. May is among the designers on the show.
