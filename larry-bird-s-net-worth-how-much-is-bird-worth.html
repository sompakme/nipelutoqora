Larry Bird Biography and NBA Career

Larry Bird was born in West Baden Springs, Indiana, on December 7, 1956. With a passion for basketball from a young age, Bird went on to become a star player at Indiana State University, leading his team to the NCAA finals in 1979.

After being drafted by the Boston Celtics in 1978, Bird went on to establish himself as one of the most dominant players in NBA history, earning three most valuable player (MVP) titles and leading the Celtics to three championships.

Throughout his career, Bird was known for his exceptional shooting ability, court vision, and competitive spirit. He was a 12-time NBA All-Star and was named to the All-NBA First Team nine times. In addition, Bird was a two-time gold medalist as a member of the US Olympic basketball team.

Some of Larry Bird’s most notable achievements include:

  • Winning the NBA Rookie of the Year award in 1980
  • Being named MVP of the NBA Finals in 1984 and 1986
  • Being named one of the 50 Greatest Players in NBA History in 1996
  • Being inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 1998

Larry Bird’s extraordinary talent and drive on the basketball court made him a true NBA legend, and his achievements have contributed significantly to his impressive net worth.
