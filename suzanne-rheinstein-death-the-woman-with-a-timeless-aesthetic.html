Suzanne Rheinstein’s death was a shock for her family and friends. The famous interior designer departed from this world on March 20th, 2023, at age 78. 

The deceased was well known for her elegant and classic aesthetic. For Suzanne, interior designing was not just a way of making money. She loved her profession profoundly and worked tirelessly to create timeless design pieces. 

She founded the design firm Suzanne Rheinstein & Associates. The firm earned a lot of recognition due to its honesty, loyalty, and uniquely crafted design pieces.

Rheinstein used her passion for designing to write books. She was a creative author and wrote several books on interior design and decorating, including “Rooms for Living: A Style for Today with Things from the Past,” “At Home: A Style for Today with Things from the Past,” and “Suzanne Rheinstein: Rooms for Living.”

Suzanne designed and transformed multiple spaces throughout her life, including private residences, hotels, and retail spaces. 

Her family, friends, and colleagues highly appreciated her talent and contribution to the field. Rheinstein’s legacy will live on through her work and the countless designers and homeowners who were inspired by her talent and vision.

Many of Rheinstein’s designs had a classic English and vintage French touch. She knew how to incorporate things of the past into today’s design requirements. 

Some sources have claimed that Suzanne lost her life to cancer. According to reports, Rheinstein was diagnosed with cancer in 2018, and she underwent treatment while continuing to work and inspire others in the design industry.
