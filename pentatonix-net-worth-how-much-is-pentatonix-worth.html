The Early Years of Pentatonix

Formed in 2011 in Arlington, Texas, Pentatonix quickly rose to fame after winning the third season of the popular reality TV series, The Sing-Off, aired on NBC. This talented a cappella group consisting of Scott Hoying, Mitch Grassi, Kirstie Maldonado, Avi Kaplan, and Kevin Olusola caught the attention of audiences and judges with their unique harmonies and breathtaking vocal performances.

“Winning The Sing-Off was a game-changer for us. It gave us the opportunity to showcase our talent and passion for music on a national platform,” says Scott Hoying, one of the founding members of Pentatonix.

As winners of the show, Pentatonix secured a recording contract with Sony Music and a cash prize of $200,000. However, their journey to success was not without obstacles. After being dropped from their label, Epic Records, the group took matters into their own hands and decided to leverage the power of the internet.

Recognizing the potential of YouTube as a platform to reach millions of viewers, Pentatonix created their own YouTube channel. They began uploading impressive a cappella covers of popular songs, captivating audiences with their creativity and flawless vocal abilities.

Today, the Pentatonix YouTube channel boasts over eight million subscribers and has amassed nearly a billion views, solidifying their status as one of the most successful musical acts to emerge from the internet age.

Albums and Achievements

Pentatonix has achieved notable success with their albums, earning critical acclaim and commercial success. Let’s take a look at some of their standout releases and achievements.

PTX, Vols. 1 & 2 (2014)

One of Pentatonix’s most successful albums is their debut studio album, PTX, Vols. 1 & 2, released in 2014. This album showcased the group’s incredible vocal talent and unique a cappella arrangements. It reached #6 on the charts in Japan, further solidifying their global appeal.

That’s Christmas to Me (2014)

In the same year, Pentatonix released their second album, “PTX.” This album continued to showcase their impressive vocal harmonies and garnered a dedicated fan base. However, it was their third album, That’s Christmas to Me, that truly set them apart. Released in 2014, it became the highest-charting holiday album in the United States since 1962. It also gained recognition as the fourth best-selling overall album that year, cementing Pentatonix’s status as a holiday favorite.

Grammy Award for Best Arrangement, Instrumental or A Cappella

Pentatonix’s exceptional talent was recognized by the prestigious Grammy Awards. In 2015, they won the Grammy Award for Best Arrangement, Instrumental or A Cappella for their incredible rendition of “Daft Punk.” This award solidified their place as one of the most accomplished and innovative acapella groups in the industry.

With their memorable albums and impressive achievements, Pentatonix continues to captivate audiences around the world with their unique sound and undeniable talent.
